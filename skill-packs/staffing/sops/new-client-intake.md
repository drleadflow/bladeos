# SOP: New Client Intake

**Owner:** CSM Agent, Closer, Chief of Staff  
**Trigger:** A new prospective client has agreed to a discovery/intake call  
**Output:** Signed client agreement + completed client profile document + first job order ready for sourcing  
**SLA:** Discovery call to first submittal in 5 business days or less

---

## Phase 1: Pre-Call Qualification (Before the Call)

Before investing 45 minutes in a discovery call, do a 10-minute qualification pass.

**Step 1 — Company Background Check**
- Google the company name + "reviews", "layoffs", "lawsuit", "Glassdoor"
- Check LinkedIn: how long has the company been active, what is headcount trajectory (growing or shrinking)?
- Check for recent news: funded? Acquired? Going through leadership changes?
- Look up the contact who booked the call on LinkedIn — what is their role, tenure, and hiring authority?

**Step 2 — Source Verification**
- How did this prospect find us? (Referral, inbound, outbound, LinkedIn)
- If a referral: note who referred them and what they were told about our services
- If cold outreach: which list/ICP did they come from? Flag any misalignment with our target verticals

**Step 3 — Pre-Call Disqualification Criteria**
Do not book the intake call if any of the following are true:
- Company has fewer than 5 employees (exception: funded startup with a clear growth plan)
- The contact does not have hiring authority and cannot confirm who does
- The role is in a geography or vertical we explicitly do not serve
- The company has an active staffing agreement with an exclusive retained firm that covers this role type

---

## Phase 2: Discovery Call (45 Minutes)

**Opening (5 min)**
- Confirm attendees and time allotted
- Set the agenda: "Today I want to understand your hiring needs and how you work so we can figure out if we are the right fit for each other — I have a few structured questions and then we will save time for yours"
- Note: framing as mutual qualification (not us selling them) sets the tone and lowers guard

**Company Overview (10 min)**
Use the `client-intake` skill for question prompts. Key data to capture:
- Industry, headcount, revenue stage, location(s)
- How the company is structured (who leads hiring decisions — CEO, VP, HR director?)
- Prior staffing agency experience: who, what happened, what would better look like?
- Payment terms expectation (introduce early — surprises at contract stage kill deals)

**Job Order Discovery (20 min)**
For each open role — capture using the `client-intake` skill:
- Role title, department, and direct manager
- Reason the role is open
- The "must-have" skills in the manager's own words (ask them to describe their best hire in this role)
- Compensation range — confirmed and approved
- Start date — hard or soft?
- Interview process: rounds, format, who is involved, typical turnaround for feedback

**Commercial Alignment (5 min)**
- Introduce fee structure: "We work on contingency for direct hire — our standard is X% of first-year salary, with a 90-day replacement guarantee. For temp placements, we charge a Y% markup on the hourly rate."
- Confirm payment terms: "We invoice on start date for direct hire, weekly for temp. Our standard terms are net-15."
- Ask directly: "Is that aligned with how you have worked with agencies before, or do we need to talk through the terms?"

**Wrap + Next Steps (5 min)**
- Summarize what you heard (repeat their job requirements back to them — this catches misalignment early)
- Confirm next step: "I will get our agreement over to you today. Once it is signed, I will begin sourcing immediately and target getting you your first submittals within 72 hours."
- Set response SLA expectation: "When I send submittals, I will include a cover note with each candidate. I find things move fastest when clients can give feedback within 48 hours — does that work for your process?"

---

## Phase 3: Post-Call Processing (Same Day)

**Step 1 — Log the Client Profile**
Within 2 hours of the call, create a client profile document using the output format from the `client-intake` skill:
- Company snapshot
- Job order details (one section per open role)
- Client risk score (GREEN / YELLOW / RED) with rationale
- Submittal strategy notes

**Step 2 — Send the Agreement**
- Use the standard staffing services agreement template
- Pre-fill with client name, fee %, payment terms, guarantee period, and role(s) discussed
- Send via DocuSign or equivalent — do not email a PDF to print and scan
- Include a one-paragraph cover note that references the call and confirms next steps
- Follow up in 24 hours if not signed — and again at 48 hours with a brief: "Happy to jump on a 10-minute call if you have any questions on the terms"

**Step 3 — Open the Job Order in the System**
Once the agreement is signed:
- Enter the job order with all fields captured on the intake call
- Set status to ACTIVE
- Assign to a recruiter (or route via the SDR based on the role type and recruiter capacity)
- Tag with client tier based on risk score and revenue potential

**Step 4 — Kickoff the Sourcing Clock**
- Set a calendar reminder for 72 hours from agreement signature — first submittal deadline
- If the intake revealed any red flags (role open >60 days, vague comp, unclear process), flag to the account manager before sourcing begins

---

## Phase 4: Qualification Standards

**Clients We Take On (GREEN baseline):**
- Clear job requirements with at least a directional comp range
- Decision-making authority identified and accessible
- Feedback SLA agreeable to 48 hours on submittals
- Willing to commit to net-30 or better payment terms
- At least 1 positive or neutral history with staffing agencies (or a clear reason they have not used one before)

**Clients That Require Senior Approval (YELLOW):**
- Role open >45 days without a hire
- Comp range is below market by 10-20% but client is willing to discuss
- Feedback history is slow (>5 days typical) but client acknowledges this and commits to improving
- Net-45 to net-60 payment terms

**Clients We Decline (RED — do not take the order without escalation):**
- Compensation is non-negotiably below market rate
- Client has had 3+ agency relationships that "all failed to deliver" — the common denominator is the client
- Unclear who makes the final hiring decision after two direct attempts to identify them
- Net-60+ payment terms with no flexibility
- Active legal dispute with a former employee related to a similar role

---

## Escalation Triggers
Escalate to the account manager or GM if:
- Client requests non-standard fee structure (below agency floor or contingent-on-trial-period)
- Client asks to modify the replacement guarantee terms significantly
- Client wants exclusivity with a retainer but the fee does not justify the commitment
- Client seems to be using the intake call to harvest candidate profiles without intent to hire

---

## Metrics to Track
- Intake call to signed agreement: target <48 hours
- Signed agreement to first submittal: target <72 hours
- Intake conversion rate: % of intake calls that result in a signed agreement (target >65%)
- Client risk distribution: track GREEN/YELLOW/RED mix quarterly — RED clients above 20% of portfolio signals business development quality issue
