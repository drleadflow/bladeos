# SOP: Placement Process

**Owner:** CSM Agent, Support Lead, Ops Manager  
**Trigger:** A candidate has been approved for submittal to a client  
**Output:** Confirmed placement through the end of the guarantee period, with a documented check-in log  
**SLA:** From job order to offer in 14 days (temp) / 30 days (perm); falloff rate <10% through guarantee

---

## Phase 1: Candidate Submittal

**Submittal Standards — The Non-Negotiables**
A submittal is not just forwarding a resume. Every submittal represents the agency's judgment. Low-quality submittals damage client trust faster than anything else.

**Step 1 — Prepare the Candidate Brief**
Do not send a resume alone. Every submittal includes a recruiter brief:
- 2-3 sentence candidate overview (their current situation, why they are looking, what they bring)
- Skills alignment: bullet the top 3 required skills and how this candidate demonstrates each
- Compensation expectation and how it aligns to the client's approved range
- Availability and proposed start date
- One honest flag: if the candidate has a gap, a job change, or anything the client will notice — name it and contextualize it. Clients who find surprises in an interview lose trust in the recruiter.

**Step 2 — Submittal Timing**
- First submittal target: 72 hours from signed client agreement for active sourcing roles
- Never submit more than 3 candidates at once — quality signal matters more than volume
- Lead with the strongest candidate; rank the others clearly in the submittal email
- For competitive roles where another agency may be involved: speed matters — aim for first submittal within 48 hours

**Step 3 — Candidate Consent**
Before submitting, confirm with the candidate:
- They have reviewed and approved the version of their resume being submitted
- They understand which company they are being submitted to (no surprise reveals during the interview)
- They confirm their compensation expectation has not changed since the phone screen
- They confirm their availability and proposed start date

## Phase 2: Interview Coordination

**Step 1 — Client Follow-Up on Submittals**
- If no feedback in 48 hours: follow up with the hiring manager directly — not the admin or HR coordinator
- Standard language: "I wanted to follow up on the submittals from [date]. Happy to provide any additional context on any of the candidates. When would you like to schedule interviews?"
- If no response in 5 business days: escalate to account manager — this is a client health signal

**Step 2 — Interview Scheduling**
- Never rely on the client and candidate to schedule directly — own the coordination
- Use a scheduling link or provide 3 time options from the client, then confirm with the candidate
- Confirm the interview details in writing to both parties: date, time, format (video/phone/in-person), location or link, interviewer name(s) and title(s), what to prepare
- Add a 15-minute buffer on your calendar at the interview start time — you will be reachable if anything goes wrong

**Step 3 — Interview Prep**
Use the `interview-prep` skill to run a prep session with the candidate at least 24 hours before the interview. Cover:
- Company research brief
- Role-specific talking points
- Likely interview questions
- Salary negotiation strategy (coach the candidate not to discuss salary directly if asked — defer to the recruiter)
- Day-of logistics

**Step 4 — Post-Interview Debrief (Same Day)**
- Call or text the candidate within 2 hours of the interview ending: "How did it go?"
- Listen for: energy level, surprises, any reservations about the role, competing offers mentioned
- Call the hiring manager or coordinator within 24 hours: "I wanted to get your initial impressions while they are fresh."
- Document both sides of the debrief — the discrepancy between candidate and client perception often reveals problems early

## Phase 3: Offer Management

**Step 1 — Receiving the Verbal Offer**
When the client communicates a verbal offer:
- Get all terms on the first call: base salary, bonus structure, equity (if any), start date, benefits summary, PTO policy, any contingencies (background check, reference check, drug screen)
- Do not relay a partial offer — gather all terms before going back to the candidate
- Confirm the offer expiration date: "How long does the candidate have to consider?"

**Step 2 — Presenting the Offer to the Candidate**
- Deliver offers by phone — never by email alone
- Present the full package, not just the number
- After presenting: pause and let them react. Do not rush to fill silence.
- If excited: "Wonderful — let me give you until [48 hours] to review the written offer and we will confirm from there."
- If hesitant: dig into the specific objection before it becomes a decline. "What would make this feel like a yes?"
- If below expectations: "I want to understand what you were hoping for so I can have the right conversation with the client." Never let the candidate negotiate directly with the client.

**Step 3 — Counter-Offer Strategy**
If the candidate received a counter-offer from their current employer:
- Do not panic — this happens. Remind them why they were looking in the first place.
- Ask: "Before this counter-offer, what was your intention?" Most candidates who were serious about leaving will reconfirm that intention when they slow down.
- Share the data: 80% of candidates who accept a counter-offer leave within 18 months. The reason they were looking has not changed; they have just been temporarily retained.
- Give them 24 hours to decide — not longer. Urgency helps cut through the noise.

**Step 4 — Written Offer and Acceptance**
- Confirm the candidate has received the written offer from the client
- Confirm acceptance in writing from the candidate (text or email to the recruiter is sufficient)
- Notify the client of acceptance immediately — before end of business the day the candidate accepts
- Update the ATS: status to OFFER ACCEPTED, log start date, guaranteed end date
- Begin the placement tracking protocol immediately (see Phase 4)

## Phase 4: Placement Tracking (Offer Acceptance → Guarantee End)

Use the `placement-tracker` skill to manage all active placements. Every placement has a documented check-in log.

**Pre-Start Check-In Schedule**
| Touchpoint | Method | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| T+0 (offer accepted) | Call + email — confirm start logistics | Recruiter |
| T+24h | Call — emotional check-in | Recruiter |
| T-7 days | Call — confirm nothing has changed | Recruiter |
| T-48h | Email — first-day logistics (parking, dress code, who to ask for) | Recruiter |
| T-24h | SMS — manager name and direct line | Recruiter |
| Morning of start | SMS — "Good luck today" | Recruiter |

**Post-Start Check-In Schedule**
| Touchpoint | Method | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| Day 1 EOD | Text candidate | Recruiter |
| Day 3 | Call candidate | Recruiter |
| Day 5 (Friday) | Call candidate + email hiring manager | Recruiter |
| Day 14 | Call candidate + email hiring manager | Account Manager |
| Day 30 | Call candidate + call hiring manager | Account Manager |
| Day 45 | Text candidate | Account Manager |
| Day 60 | Call candidate + call hiring manager | Account Manager |
| Day 75 | Call candidate | Account Manager |
| Day 90 (guarantee end) | Close-out call — both parties | Account Manager |

**Placement Status Definitions**
- GREEN: All check-ins current, no flags from either side
- YELLOW: Missed check-in, slow client response, or vague candidate sentiment — increase contact frequency to every 5 days
- RED: Active falloff risk — escalate to senior recruiter within 24 hours
- REPLACED: Falloff occurred; replacement process initiated within 48 hours per guarantee terms
- COMPLETE: Guarantee period expired with placement confirmed solid; relationship handed to account management for next job order

---

## Phase 5: Falloff Protocol

A falloff is when a candidate does not start, leaves, or is terminated within the guarantee period. Every falloff is a financial and relationship event — handle it fast.

**Within 24 hours of a falloff:**
1. Contact the candidate: understand what happened without assigning blame
2. Contact the client: acknowledge the situation, do not be defensive, confirm your commitment to the guarantee
3. Activate the replacement clause: confirm the timeline and terms in the agreement
4. Open a replacement job order immediately — do not wait for the client to ask

**Root Cause Analysis:**
- Document the root cause in the ATS (candidate-initiated, client-initiated, external — layoff, offer rescinded, etc.)
- Tag any contributing factors: misaligned expectations, prep gap, comp issue, start-date pressure, competing offer accepted
- Review for patterns quarterly — if >15% of falloffs share the same root cause, the process has a structural problem

**Replacement Strategy:**
- Pull from the existing pipeline first — a held candidate from the original submittal is often the fastest path
- If the pipeline is thin, reopen active sourcing immediately
- Deliver replacement submittals within 72 hours of the falloff confirmation
- Do not use the falloff as a reason to renegotiate fee terms — honor the guarantee, preserve the relationship

---

## Phase 6: Guarantee Period Close-Out

When the guarantee period ends successfully:

**Day 90 Call Agenda (both candidate and client, separate calls):**
- Candidate: "How are things going? Is the role what you expected? Any concerns on the horizon?"
- Client: "We are at the 90-day mark — how is [candidate name] performing? Anything we can do to support their continued success here?"
- Both: Ask for a referral — "Who else in your network is either a great candidate or a company that could use our help?"
- Client: Ask for the next job order — "Now that this one is filled, what else is coming up on your hiring plan?"

**Handoff to Account Management:**
- Transfer relationship ownership to the account manager with a full placement summary
- Set a 90-day account check-in reminder
- Log the client's upcoming hiring needs in the ATS for proactive outreach

---

## Metrics to Track
- Time-to-fill: from job order open to offer accepted (target: <14 days temp, <30 days perm)
- Submittal-to-interview ratio: target 3:1
- Interview-to-placement ratio: target 3:1
- Falloff rate through guarantee: target <10%
- Pre-start falloff rate: track separately — high rate signals offer management or comp alignment problem
- Replacement delivery time: target <72 hours from falloff to first replacement submittal
- Day 90 NPS from client and candidate: target >8/10
