# SOP: Candidate Screening

**Owner:** SDR, Closer, Nurture Engine  
**Trigger:** A candidate has applied to a job order, been sourced from a database, or been referred  
**Output:** Scored candidate with a go/hold/pass decision and a recruiter brief ready for submittal  
**SLA:** From first contact to go/no-go decision in 48 hours or less

---

## Phase 1: Pre-Screen Research (Before the Call)

Spend 5 minutes on a candidate before calling them. Cold-calling a candidate without knowing anything about their background is a waste of both parties' time and signals that your agency is not serious.

**Step 1 — Profile Review**
- Read the resume fully: look for career trajectory, not just titles
- Check LinkedIn: does it match the resume? Note any discrepancies — gaps, different job titles, or companies that appear on LinkedIn but not the resume
- Google the candidate's name + "LinkedIn" if you do not have a resume — understand their footprint
- Note the most recent 2-3 positions: what was the company size, what was the scope of their role, what were likely reasons for each departure?

**Step 2 — Source Context**
- How did this candidate enter the pipeline? (Applied, sourced, referral, reactivated from database)
- If from a prior placement or previous submission: what is their history with the agency? Did they ghost, withdraw, or decline an offer before?
- If referral: who referred them and do they carry that person's endorsement?

**Step 3 — Role Alignment Check**
Before calling, assess on paper:
- Does the resume titles/skills obviously match the job order requirements? (If clearly no, send a courtesy email not a call — do not waste the phone screen)
- Is the candidate's most recent comp visible or estimable? Will there be a salary misalignment?
- Is their location compatible with the role (remote/hybrid/onsite)?

---

## Phase 2: Phone Screen (20-25 Minutes)

Structure every phone screen the same way. Consistent screening = comparable data across all candidates.

**Opening (2 min)**
- Introduce yourself and the agency
- Confirm you have 20 minutes and state the purpose: "I want to learn about your background, share a role I think could be a strong fit, and figure out together if it makes sense to take it further"
- Ask: "Is now still a good time?"

**Current Situation (5 min)**
- "Walk me through what you are doing right now — give me the headline on your current role."
- What are their actual responsibilities day-to-day? (Compare to title — many people are under-titling or over-titling)
- "Are you actively looking, or are you open to the right opportunity?" (Distinguishes urgency — active candidates move faster but may have more competing offers)
- "What is your current compensation, all in?" (Base, bonus, equity — get the full picture before discussing the role's comp)

**Career Narrative (5 min)**
- "Walk me through the last few moves on your resume — what was behind each transition?"
- Listen for: voluntary vs involuntary departures, pattern of growth or stagnation, stability vs job-hopping, whether they take ownership of career decisions
- Flag: any departure described as "they restructured" or "it just wasn't a fit" without elaboration — follow up with "What specifically led to that?"
- Ask about the most recent departure specifically: "What is making you open to a new role right now?"

**Skills Assessment (5 min)**
- Pull the top 3 required skills from the job order and verify directly:
  - "The role requires [specific skill]. How much hands-on experience do you have with that?"
  - "Give me an example of a time you used that in a high-stakes situation."
  - "What tools or systems have you used for [specific function]?"
- Note: do not ask if they have a skill; ask them to evidence it. "Do you know Excel?" always gets a yes.

**Motivation & Fit Check (3 min)**
- "What does your ideal next role look like?" (Does the job order match what they want?)
- "What would make you say no to an opportunity?" (Uncovers hidden requirements — location, equity, remote flexibility, title)
- "How are you thinking about timing?" (Confirms availability and urgency)

**Role Presentation (3 min)**
- Present the role only after you have learned about the candidate — not before
- Frame it as a match: "Based on what you've told me, here is why I think this could be interesting for you..."
- Cover: company overview (30 seconds), role scope, comp range, and key differentiators
- End with: "Does that sound like something worth exploring?"

**Close (2 min)**
- If interested: "Great — I will send you the full details by EOD and get your consent to share your profile with the client. If the client confirms interest, we will move into interview prep."
- If not interested: "No problem. Can I ask what would make this more interesting? And are there other types of roles where I should keep you in mind?"
- In both cases: ask for two referrals — "Is there anyone in your network who might be a strong fit for this or similar roles?"

## Phase 3: Candidate Scoring

After the phone screen, use the `candidate-scorer` skill to produce a formal score and submittal decision. Complete within 1 hour of the call while details are fresh.

Score across 5 dimensions (each 1-5):
1. Skills Match
2. Experience Level
3. Availability
4. Culture Fit
5. Salary Alignment

**Go Decision (composite 17+):** Proceed to submittal prep immediately.  
**Hold Decision (composite 12-16):** Identify the one gap. Can it be resolved with a follow-up question or client conversation? If yes, resolve it before submitting. If no, bench the candidate for a better-fit role.  
**Pass Decision (composite <12):** Do not submit. Send a courteous hold email — do not ghost candidates.

## Phase 4: Reference Checks

Run references for TIER 1 candidates before submittal to a new client, and always before offer stage for direct hire roles.

**Reference Protocol:**
- Request 2-3 professional references minimum — at least 1 must be a former direct manager
- "Personal references" are not accepted — colleagues, peers, or reports only if a manager reference is impossible
- Contact references by phone, not email — email gets screened; phone gets answered
- Standard questions for each reference:
  1. "How did you work with [candidate] and for how long?"
  2. "What were [candidate]'s strongest contributions in their role?"
  3. "What was an area where [candidate] was working to improve?"
  4. "Would you rehire [candidate] if the opportunity arose? Why or why not?"
  5. "Is there anything you would want the next employer to know about working with [candidate]?"
- Red flag: any reference who pauses before answering #4 or gives a non-answer

**When to Skip References Pre-Submittal:**
- The client has requested a quick submittal and will run their own references post-offer
- The candidate is for a temp/contract role under 90 days
- The candidate was a direct referral from a trusted source who has already vouched

---

## Phase 5: Red Flag Review

Before finalizing any submittal, review the Red Flag Checklist. Two or more flags = escalate to senior recruiter before submitting.

- [ ] Unexplained employment gap >6 months in last 5 years
- [ ] Three or more jobs in three years without a pattern (layoffs, contracting, acquisition)
- [ ] Compensation expectation increased from what they stated on the phone screen
- [ ] References unavailable or the reason given is vague
- [ ] Candidate is actively interviewing with 5+ other companies simultaneously
- [ ] Start date has shifted more than once
- [ ] Evasive answers about reason for leaving current role
- [ ] LinkedIn profile and resume do not match in material ways (dates, titles, companies)
- [ ] Background check concern they did not proactively disclose

---

## Phase 6: Candidate Communication Standards

**After a phone screen (same day):**
- Send a recap email confirming what was discussed, the role details, and next steps
- Get written consent to share their profile with the client

**If not moving forward:**
- Send a clear, respectful hold or pass email within 24 hours — do not leave candidates hanging
- Standard hold language: "At this time we do not have a role that aligns perfectly with your background, but we are actively working new orders and will be in touch as soon as something matches."
- Never ghost a candidate — the staffing industry's reputation is already strained; we do not make it worse

**Pipeline Maintenance:**
- Add all screened candidates to the appropriate stage in the ATS
- Tag skills, industries, and comp range for future searchability
- Set a re-engagement reminder for hold candidates at 60 days — markets and roles change

---

## Metrics to Track
- Screen-to-submittal rate: % of screened candidates submitted (target: 30-40% — below 20% signals too many unqualified inbounds; above 60% signals standards are too low)
- Submittal-to-interview rate: target 3:1 (3 submittals per client interview scheduled)
- Time from candidate contact to go/no-go decision: target <48 hours
- Candidate ghosting rate after phone screen: track quarterly — high rate signals follow-up lag
